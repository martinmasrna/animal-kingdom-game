# Handoff 03 — Future card-design backlog

You are a systems-minded card designer. Build a small ranked backlog of **future design work**.
This is paper design only: do not change cards, engine, effects, configuration, tests, or the active run.

## Read first

1. `CLAUDE.md`
2. `docs/STATUS.md`
3. `docs/rules/mental-model.md`
4. `docs/cards/backlog.md`
5. `docs/cards/decks/README.md`
6. `docs/cards/card-candidates.md`
7. `docs/cards/expansion-design-todo.md`
8. `docs/balance/aggro-redesign.md`

## Boundaries

The canonical pool is content-complete as seven premade 4-4-6 decks. Landmarks were cut on
2026-07-15; do not revive Landmark-only designs as near-term work. Balance is being re-baselined,
so stale win-rate reads do not prove a card needs tuning. A candidate remains explicitly provisional.

## Task

Identify valuable future card-design problems—not merely flashy cards. Favor missing roles,
archetype pressure points, interaction gaps, and sidegrades that create deckbuilding choices. Avoid
generic value cards that improve open-construction good-stuff.

For every candidate use the body (Lion 7 ground / Eagle 5 Flight), card, action, delay,
floor/ceiling, and closest-existing-card ledgers. Respect the one-action economy and 4-4-6 shape.

## Required output

Write `docs/cards/future-card-design-session-YYYY-MM-DD.md` with:

- Five to eight ranked design problems.
- For the top three, two candidate directions each: intended home, fence, payoff, counterplay, and ledgers.
- A table of candidates invalidated or needing re-cast because Landmarks are gone.
- A "do not design yet" list with the data/ruling/identity blocker.
- A next prototype wave of at most four cards, conditional on the simulation finishing.

Keep names and strengths provisional. Do not add candidates directly to `card-candidates.md`.

## Done when

A later session can choose a small prototype wave with clear reasons, guardrails, and risks.
