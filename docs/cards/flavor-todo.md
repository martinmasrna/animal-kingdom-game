# Card Flavor and Names — To-Do

Focused follow-up to `decks/flavor-review.md`, including consequences of the July balance review.
No name below is locked unless explicitly marked in another source-of-truth document.
All mechanical card candidates are inventoried in [`card-candidates.md`](card-candidates.md);
this document contains only their unresolved names, species, and flavor questions.

## 1. Required by recent rarity/card changes

### 1.0 Tribe-label symmetry: Cat/Dog versus Feline/Canine

- [ ] Choose a deliberate player-facing pair:
  - **Cat / Canine:** keeps the familiar current Cat label but remains grammatically asymmetric.
  - **Feline / Canine:** accessible, parallel adjectives, and broad enough for lions/foxes.
  - **Felid / Canid:** taxonomically clean parallel nouns, but less immediately friendly.
- [ ] If `Cat` remains, confirm that the asymmetry is intentional rather than inherited drift.

### 1.1 Stoop becomes a generic rare Bird

- [ ] Recommended identity: **Secretarybird**.
  - It is a real bird famous for killing dangerous prey on the ground.
  - The existing candidate slate already pairs it with the exact proposed line:
    `Bird · STR 4 · Flight. Battlecry: remove an adjacent enemy of strength 4 or less.`
  - It reads naturally in Egg Control's Bird/Snake ecosystem as a predator among snakes.
- [ ] Alternatives if Secretarybird feels too anti-Snake for the mixed tribe: **Goshawk,
  Harrier, Kite**.
- [ ] Retire “Stoop” as the card name when the rarity change ships. It reads as an individual
  legendary name rather than a generic species.

### 1.2 Goliath becomes a generic rare Snake

The common **Anaconda** means a rare giant anaconda would violate the one-species rule now that
Goliath no longer has the legendary named-individual exemption.

- [ ] Recommended identity: **Titanoboa**.
  - Real extinct snake, not a mythic creature.
  - Its extraordinary size fits `strength = number of removed units`.
  - Exotic enough to read as Rare while remaining a generic species.
- [ ] Alternative: **African Rock Python**, provided no generic Python is selected elsewhere.
- [ ] Avoid Green Anaconda/Burmese Anaconda variants; they preserve or blur the collision with
  common Anaconda.

### 1.3 Black Swan becomes legendary

“Black Swan” is a species/description, not an individual legendary name.

- [ ] Shortlist:
  - **Nocturne, the Black Swan** — elegant, dark, and disruptive.
  - **Portent, the Black Swan** — directly evokes the unexpected bad event.
  - **Calamity, the Black Swan** — louder and more playful; fits the random hand loss.
- [ ] Recommended: **Nocturne** if the Egg deck should remain lyrical; **Portent** if effect
  legibility matters more.
- [ ] Keep the animal a real black swan. The “black swan event” is metaphorical flavor, not a
  supernatural creature.

### 1.4 New Aggro cover-retaliation legendary

The effect wants an animal known for aerial speed or evasive maneuvering:

> STR 4 · Flight. The first time an enemy unit covers this, return that enemy to its owner's hand.

- [ ] Recommended concept: **Slipstream, the Needletail**.
  - White-throated needletails are exceptionally fast level-flight birds.
  - “Slipstream” makes the failed interception legible without citing a myth.
- [ ] Alternatives:
  - **Gale, the Black Kite** — predatory and maneuverable, slightly more martial.
  - **Ricochet, the Swallow** — playful and perfectly evasive, but less majestic.
  - **Zephyr, the Alpine Swift** — elegant, though “Zephyr” brushes mythological naming.
- [ ] Ensure the art shows the attacker overshooting as the legendary bird folds away from the
  cover, so the bounce effect reads visually.

### 1.5 Aggro Hornet placeholder

The new version has Flight and consumes another copy from hand/deck to destroy an adjacent enemy.

- [ ] Do **not** rename it simply “Wasp”; Colony already has Worker Wasp.
- [ ] Recommended working species: **Tarantula Hawk**.
  - It flies, has an exceptionally potent sting, and is already the cleanest collision-free
    candidate in the earlier flavor audit.
  - The “remove another copy” cost is mechanical abstraction rather than literal self-sacrifice.
- [ ] Reject **Velvet Ant** for this version: the stinging females are wingless, conflicting with
  the newly added Flight keyword.
- [ ] Consider **Spider Wasp** if Tarantula Hawk feels too specific, but collision-check the wider
  Wasp naming rule first.

### 1.6 Vulture versus Crow

- [ ] Decide whether **Vulture**, **Crow**, **Rook**, or another Bird carries:
  `Flight. Battlecry: draw 1; if an enemy unit was removed this turn, draw 2 instead.`
- [ ] Alternative to compare: reserve **Vulture** for a separate literal consumption/scavenging
  (“eat”) identity.

### 1.7 Copy/mimic animal

- [ ] Compare the animal fit for two possible mechanical scopes:
  - **Parrot, mockingbird, or lyrebird:** repeats a sound, so it should copy/repeat a Battlecry.
  - **Mimic Octopus or cuttlefish:** changes physical appearance/behavior, so it better fits a
    full copy of strength, tags, keywords, and rules text.
- [ ] If the full-copy card remains a Parrot, ensure the art sells imitation rather than literal
  transformation.
- [ ] A legendary Parrot needs an individual name; defer naming until copy timing and effect
  scope are locked.

### 1.8 Dog/Canine copy-scaling card

- [ ] Decide whether the STR 3 “give every other copy +3 wherever it is” design reworks the
  existing **Dog** or belongs to another Canine.
- [ ] If it is a separate card, choose an identity that does not violate the one-species rule.
- [ ] Art should show littermates/pack members visibly maturing with each arrival, making the
  wherever-they-are buffs legible.

## 2. Legendary naming policy

The present pool mixes original evocative names (`Eon`, `Ember`, `Vesper`) with direct literary
or historical references (`Raksha`, `Falstaff`, `Carmilla`, `Scrooge`, `Methuselah`, `Lobo`).
Both can be charming, but the line is currently implicit.

- [ ] Choose one policy before final flavor-lock:
  1. **Evocative-original:** names may echo literature/folklore but should not directly reuse a
     famous character.
  2. **Literary menagerie:** direct literary/historical names are allowed when the animal/effect
     fit is unusually strong.
- [ ] Recommendation: choose **Literary menagerie**, capped at roughly one conspicuous wink per
  deck. Falstaff and Scrooge give the game welcome humor; twenty-eight allusions would feel like
  a trivia set.
- [ ] Require every legendary to identify a real species in its art/flavor note, even when the
  printed name is abstract.
- [ ] Legendary names should pass three tests: speakable aloud, distinct at a glance, and
  inseparable from the card's effect after one game.

## 4. Species and tag cleanup

- [ ] Formally allow repeated species for **Colony castes only**. Do not generalize that exception
  to domestic dog breeds or arbitrary subspecies.
- [ ] Pin Black Panther as a melanistic **leopard**, not jaguar.
- [ ] Make `Fish` an active tag.
- [ ] Decide whether both tortoises are Megafauna or whether both are tagless. If Jonathan remains
  a Ramp tortoise, Megafauna is defensible for that legendary individual; the generic Food OTK
  Giant Tortoise can remain tagless.
- [ ] For expansion Canines, use genuinely distinct wild species (Maned Wolf, Bat-eared Fox,
  Raccoon Dog, Fennec Fox, Ethiopian Wolf, Culpeo). Do not treat dog breeds as separate species.
- [ ] Treat extinct real animals such as Titanoboa and dinosaurs as animals, not invented
  creatures. Mark extinct status in flavor notes so the theme boundary stays explicit.

## 5. Flavor-first art prompts to preserve mechanics

- [ ] **Secretarybird:** a precise downward kick toward small prey; the bird remains airborne or
  just landing, supporting Flight plus capped removal.
- [ ] **Titanoboa:** coils formed from silhouettes of removed animals; its size visibly grows with
  the Remove Pile.
- [ ] **Black Swan legendary:** a calm dark bird whose arrival sends a flock scattering, showing
  hand disruption without magical effects.
- [ ] **Aggro retaliation legendary:** an enemy lunges into empty air while the bird snaps into a
  new flight line.
- [ ] **Burrow:** show displaced soil and a distant emergence point; do not depict teleportation.
- [ ] **Venom:** use visible bite/sting marks and a delayed weakening pose, not instant magical
  disintegration.
- [ ] **Landmarks:** keep them ecological—nests, reefs, caves, trees, carcasses, mounds—not human
  buildings or abstract spell objects.
